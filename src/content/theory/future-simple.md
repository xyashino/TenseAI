## Usage

The Future Simple tense (with "will") is used to describe:

- **Spontaneous decisions**: Decisions made at the moment of speaking

  - _I'm thirsty. I'll have some water._
  - _The phone is ringing. I'll answer it._

- **Predictions**: Things we think will happen in the future

  - _It will rain tomorrow._
  - _She will be a great doctor._

- **Promises and offers**: Commitments or offers to do something

  - _I'll help you with your homework._
  - _We'll be there on time._

- **Future facts**: Things that will definitely happen

  - _The sun will rise at 6:30 AM tomorrow._
  - _I'll be 30 next year._

- **Threats and warnings**: Negative consequences
  - _If you don't study, you'll fail the exam._
  - _Be careful, or you'll fall!_

## Sentence Construction

### Affirmative Sentences

**Structure**: Subject + will + base verb

| Subject                 | Auxiliary Verb | Base Verb | Example                |
| ----------------------- | -------------- | --------- | ---------------------- |
| I/You/He/She/It/We/They | will ('ll)     | call      | _I'll call you later._ |

**Examples**:

- _I will call you later._
- _She will arrive at 5 PM._
- _They will help us move._

### Negative Sentences

**Structure**: Subject + will not (won't) + base verb

| Subject                 | Auxiliary Verb   | Base Verb | Example            |
| ----------------------- | ---------------- | --------- | ------------------ |
| I/You/He/She/It/We/They | will not (won't) | be        | _I won't be late._ |

**Examples**:

- _I won't be late._
- _He won't forget your birthday._
- _They won't come to the party._

### Questions

**Structure**: Will + subject + base verb?

| Auxiliary Verb | Subject      | Base Verb         | Example                         |
| -------------- | ------------ | ----------------- | ------------------------------- |
| Will           | you/she/they | come/finish/bring | _Will you come to the meeting?_ |

**Examples**:

- _Will you come to the meeting?_
- _Will she finish on time?_
- _Will they bring the documents?_

## Common Time Expressions

Future Simple is often used with these time expressions:

- **General future**: tomorrow, soon, later, next week/month/year, in the future

  - _I'll see you tomorrow._
  - _She'll call you soon._
  - _They'll visit us next month._

- **Specific future times**: tonight, this evening, in an hour/a week/a month

  - _We'll meet tonight._
  - _The package will arrive in two days._

- **Conditional expressions**: if, when, as soon as, before, after
  - _If it rains, we'll stay home._
  - _I'll call you when I arrive._
  - _We'll start as soon as everyone is here._

## Other Ways to Express Future

### "Going to" for planned actions

Use "going to" for plans or intentions decided before the moment of speaking:

- _I'm going to study medicine._ (planned decision)
- _They're going to get married next year._ (already decided)

### Present Continuous for arranged future events

Use Present Continuous for arrangements with specific details:

- _I'm meeting John at 3 PM._ (arranged meeting)
- _We're flying to Paris tomorrow._ (booked flight)

### Present Simple for scheduled events

Use Present Simple for fixed timetables and schedules:

- _The train leaves at 9:30 AM._
- _The concert starts at 8 PM._

---

**Tip**: The choice between "will" and "going to" depends on whether the decision is spontaneous (will) or already planned (going to):

- Spontaneous: _It's cold in here. I'll close the window._
- Planned: _I'm going to close the window later. It's too noisy outside right now._
