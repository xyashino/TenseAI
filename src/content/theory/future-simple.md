# Usage

The Future Simple tense (with "will") is used to describe:

- **Spontaneous decisions**: Decisions made at the moment of speaking
  - *I'm thirsty. I'll have some water.*
  - *The phone is ringing. I'll answer it.*

- **Predictions**: Things we think will happen in the future
  - *It will rain tomorrow.*
  - *She will be a great doctor.*

- **Promises and offers**: Commitments or offers to do something
  - *I'll help you with your homework.*
  - *We'll be there on time.*

- **Future facts**: Things that will definitely happen
  - *The sun will rise at 6:30 AM tomorrow.*
  - *I'll be 30 next year.*

- **Threats and warnings**: Negative consequences
  - *If you don't study, you'll fail the exam.*
  - *Be careful, or you'll fall!*

# Sentence Construction

## Affirmative Sentences

**Structure**: Subject + will + base verb

- I/You/He/She/It/We/They **will** (I'll/You'll/He'll/She'll/It'll/We'll/They'll) + base verb

**Examples**:
- *I will call you later.*
- *She will arrive at 5 PM.*
- *They will help us move.*

## Negative Sentences

**Structure**: Subject + will not (won't) + base verb

**Examples**:
- *I won't be late.*
- *He won't forget your birthday.*
- *They won't come to the party.*

## Questions

**Structure**: Will + subject + base verb?

**Examples**:
- *Will you come to the meeting?*
- *Will she finish on time?*
- *Will they bring the documents?*

# Common Time Expressions

Future Simple is often used with these time expressions:

- **General future**: tomorrow, soon, later, next week/month/year, in the future
  - *I'll see you tomorrow.*
  - *She'll call you soon.*
  - *They'll visit us next month.*

- **Specific future times**: tonight, this evening, in an hour/a week/a month
  - *We'll meet tonight.*
  - *The package will arrive in two days.*

- **Conditional expressions**: if, when, as soon as, before, after
  - *If it rains, we'll stay home.*
  - *I'll call you when I arrive.*
  - *We'll start as soon as everyone is here.*

# Other Ways to Express Future

## "Going to" for planned actions

Use "going to" for plans or intentions decided before the moment of speaking:

- *I'm going to study medicine.* (planned decision)
- *They're going to get married next year.* (already decided)

## Present Continuous for arranged future events

Use Present Continuous for arrangements with specific details:

- *I'm meeting John at 3 PM.* (arranged meeting)
- *We're flying to Paris tomorrow.* (booked flight)

## Present Simple for scheduled events

Use Present Simple for fixed timetables and schedules:

- *The train leaves at 9:30 AM.*
- *The concert starts at 8 PM.*

---

**Tip**: The choice between "will" and "going to" depends on whether the decision is spontaneous (will) or already planned (going to):

- Spontaneous: *It's cold in here. I'll close the window.*
- Planned: *I'm going to close the window later. It's too noisy outside right now.*
