# Usage

The Past Simple tense is used to describe:

- **Completed actions in the past**: Actions that started and finished at a specific time
  - _I visited Paris last year._
  - _She graduated in 2020._

- **Sequential past events**: A series of actions that happened one after another
  - _He woke up, had breakfast, and left for work._
  - _They arrived, checked in, and went to their room._

- **Past habits and repeated actions**: Regular actions in the past (often with "used to" or "would")
  - _I played football every weekend when I was young._
  - _She always wore a hat._

- **Past states**: Situations that were true in the past but not anymore
  - _I lived in Tokyo for three years._
  - _He worked as a teacher before becoming a writer._

# Sentence Construction

## Affirmative Sentences

**Structure**: Subject + past form of verb

**Regular verbs**: add -ed to the base form

- work → worked
- play → played
- study → studied

**Irregular verbs**: use the specific past form

- go → went
- have → had
- see → saw

**Examples**:

- _I walked to the office yesterday._
- _She wrote a book last year._
- _They bought a new house in 2022._

## Negative Sentences

**Structure**: Subject + did not (didn't) + base verb

**Examples**:

- _I didn't go to the party._
- _He didn't finish his homework._
- _They didn't see the movie._

## Questions

**Structure**: Did + subject + base verb?

**Examples**:

- _Did you enjoy the concert?_
- _Did she call you yesterday?_
- _Did they visit the museum?_

# Common Time Expressions

Past Simple is often used with these time expressions:

- **Specific past times**: yesterday, last night/week/month/year, ago
  - _I saw him yesterday._
  - _She moved here three years ago._

- **Dates and times**: in 2020, on Monday, at 3 PM
  - _We met in 2018._
  - _The event happened on Tuesday._

- **Past time periods**: when I was young, during my childhood, in those days
  - _When I was young, I loved reading._
  - _During my childhood, we lived in the countryside._
